- **Remarks**: The dog had been successfully treated on a regular basis for 2,5 years with various azole antifungal agents to control clinical signs due to Malassezia dermatitis/otitis. In the last months the dog had begun to not respond any more to treatment. Accordingly, the appearance of resistance phenomena was suspected. The isolate was tested for its susceptibility to various antifungal agents (itraconazole, ketoconazole, miconazole etc.). The MICs of some azole agents were higher than MICs obtained for a reference strain (CBS 1879) or other field isolates. All these findings support the possibility that this isolate had acquired antifungal resistance
